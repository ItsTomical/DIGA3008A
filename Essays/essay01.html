Essay 1: UX & UI Analysis

UI, UX, and Ethical Design: A Look at the SANParks Website

21 April 2025

A user experience and interface analysis of the South African National Parks website. This essay explores how design choices affect usability, fairness, and access, and reflects on what ethical interaction design looks like in the public sector.

Introduction

Today, websites are a big part of how we connect with services and information. The way a website looks (user interface or UI) and how easy it is to use (user experience or UX) shape our experience online. These design choices don’t just affect how we move around a site, they also raise important ethical issues such as fairness, privacy, and accessibility. Effective interaction design directly influences the level of trust and control users feel when using a website. As Mok (2025) states, “Trust is the invisible foundation of every great user experience.”

This essay explores the UI, UX, and ethical challenges of the South African National Parks (SANParks) website, which plays a crucial role in tourism, education, and conservation in South Africa. It looks at how the website works, how its UI and UX affect usability, and whether its design follows ethical web standards such as accessibility, transparency, and empowering users. At its core, ethical UX means designing with the user’s best interests in mind: protecting privacy, supporting informed choices, and creating respectful, inclusive digital experiences (Mok, 2023).

Background

The SANParks website was updated in 2023 (SANParks, 2025, p.25), with clear improvements in how it looks and works. The changes were made to better serve different types of users, especially tourists and people interested in nature and conservation.

SANParks, which stands for South African National Parks, is a public entity responsible for managing 21 national parks across South Africa. It plays a key role in protecting the country’s biodiversity and supporting sustainable tourism (SANParks, 2025). The SANParks website is an important tool that provides the public with easy access to information about the parks, allows for booking accommodation and activities, and promotes responsible tourism practices. It serves as a digital link between the government, local communities, and both domestic and international visitors (SANParks, 2025). As a public entity, SANParks has a duty to serve the public interest, making the ethical design of its website particularly important. National parks help promote shared heritage, support rural development, and protect natural and cultural resources (SANParks, 2025). Because the SANParks website connects people to these public benefits, its website must deliver services effectively and support inclusive, user-friendly interaction.

UI and UX: Structure, Navigation, and Experience

The SANParks website is a good example of how a thoughtfully designed UI and UX can work together to create a site that is easy to use, works well, and keeps people engaged. Since it connects the public with a national conservation body, the website is designed to reflect the organisation’s goals and values.

The UI is visually clean, consistent, and aligns with SANParks’ brand as a conservation authority. It uses a natural colour palette, with earth tones such as green, orange and brown reinforcing the site’s environmental theme and setting a nature-focused tone. High-quality images of landscapes, wildlife, and parks are used throughout the site, making it visually appealing and adding emotional and contextual depth. This combination of colour, imagery, and layout helps guide users through the site in a persuasive but respectful way, encouraging them to explore without crossing into manipulation, which happens “when it infringes on user autonomy, forcing or tricking users into making decisions they wouldn’t normally make” (Mok, 2023).

The homepage layout and main navigation are important parts of the UI. A top navigation bar shows clear headings like Travel, Conservation, Socio-Economic Transformation, Corporate, and Contact. These remain fixed as users scroll, making it easier to see and find things. Each heading has dropdown menus that lead to specific content such as park details, planning your trip, booking options, cultural heritage, vacancies, and donation pages. These features create a clear and easy-to-use structure that helps users easily find what they need.

In terms of UX, the site provides users with a seamless and efficient experience. The booking system is a key feature. Users can search for availability, select accommodation, book guided activities, and make secure payments without having to create an account. This design gives users control and improves ease of use, following usability principles like control and efficiency (Nielsen, 1994). It also allows users to change dates, add activities like game drives, and make payments with fewer steps, making it more user-friendly.

The website’s booking system uses RoomSeeker™ (KRS, 2024), an accommodation booking and property management platform developed by Real Travel and Tourism Systems (RTTS). This system allows SANParks to manage real-time availability across multiple channels, including online platforms, the call centre, and walk-ins. It's designed to handle high demand during peak seasons without slowing down. Importantly, the system safely manages visitor information and follows the Protection of Personal Information Act (POPIA) (KRS, 2024), building trust and ensuring ethical data use.

The website’s information architecture plays an important role in UX and helps improve its usability. Content is organised around key user goals such as Plan a Trip, Explore Parks, and Get Involved. This structure makes it easier for users to find what they need based on their goal. The design uses progressive disclosure, meaning that basic information is shown first and then more detailed options are revealed as users go deeper into the site. This helps lower cognitive overload and keeps the experience simple and focused (Craig, n.d.).

Interactive UI elements like call-to-action buttons, icons, and hover effects are used consistently and work well. Buttons are placed next to related content, are brightly coloured to stand out, and clearly show the next steps in the user journey. For example, the Book Your Trip button at the top right of the Home Page is bright orange and stands out from the rest of the page. The search button uses the same colour, making it easy to find. Icons and hover effects also help guide the user’s attention and show what actions they can take.

Together, these UI and UX features show a thoughtful and ethical approach to interaction design. They support transparency by clearly showcasing available services, give users control with simple tasks and secure systems, and respect their time and attention. The SANParks website, through its visual design and user-friendly layout, is a good example of how digital tools can support public services and meet the needs of local and international users.

Mobile Responsiveness and Accessibility

About 98% of people worldwide used their mobile phones to access the internet in the third quarter of 2024 (Petrosyan, 2025). This makes it very important for a website to work well on mobile devices to give users a good experience (UX). The SANParks website adjusts well to smaller screens, with flexible images, menus that can collapse, and text that is easy to read. This mobile-friendly design gives users a smooth experience on their phones without needing a separate mobile app.

However, accessibility on the website (both desktop and mobile), especially for users with disabilities, could be better. It does use high-contrast colours and text that can be resized, but it is missing important accessibility features like screen reader support or assisted technologies, alternative text for all images, and an accessibility toggle. Not having these features goes against the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1, which recommend that websites should be usable by everyone, including people with vision or motor impairments (W3C, 2024).

From an ethical point of view, this is an issue of digital inclusion. As a public sector website, SANParks should make sure that everyone can use its services, especially for important things like bookings or learning about conservation. If the site is not designed for accessibility, it can make it harder for users with disabilities to use it independently.

Transparency and Trust

Interaction design is not only about usability but also about the values built into digital systems. The ethical aspects of interaction design include data privacy, transparency in system behaviour, and support for informed decision-making (Aparna, 2024).

The SANParks website has some ethical strengths. It doesn’t ask for unnecessary personal information for tasks like browsing park details or checking prices. This shows respect for user privacy and reduces data exposure. Additionally, booking forms clearly display prices, park rules, and cancellation policies, promoting transparency and helping users make informed choices.

The site also has a pop-up cookie policy and links to its privacy and cookie policies in the footer. However, it could be better by adding a clear, POPIA-compliant banner on the home page. As data protection rules like South Africa’s POPIA become more important, users should easily see how their data is collected and used. Adding this kind of banner would be a simple but important ethical improvement.

User empowerment

Interaction design should give users control, clear choices, and feedback during their journey. On the SANParks website, most interactions are straightforward and easy to follow. For example, when booking accommodation, users get clear feedback, like “date unavailable” or “price updated”, and can change details without starting over. Clear feedback is important because it keeps users informed, reduces user confusion, builds confidence, and makes the experience feel smooth and responsive (Agarwal, 2024). When users see immediate responses to their actions, they feel more in control and are less likely to make mistakes. The SANParks site supports this by providing messages that guide users without overwhelming them.

Interaction Design in a Public Sector Context

The SANParks website works well in areas like user interface, mobile responsiveness, and clear park information, helping with tourism and access to services. However, it could do more to support values like fairness and inclusion. The site lacks important accessibility features for users with disabilities, which is a big issue for a public sector platform that should be available to everyone. These accessibility gaps go against the values of fairness and inclusion that SANParks stands for. While the site is easy to use and looks good, it could improve in making sure all users have equal access and meet higher standards of inclusion.

Recommendations

The SANParks website works well overall, but a few changes could make it easier to use and more ethical. It could improve accessibility by adding screen reader support, alternative text for images, and an accessibility toggle. The site already has a pop-up cookie policy and links to its privacy and cookie policies in the footer, but it could add a clear, POPIA-compliant banner on the homepage. With data protection laws like South Africa’s POPIA, users should easily see how their data is being collected and used. Offering more South African language options would help make the site more inclusive. The website also has a map showing park locations and nearby airports, but adding features like virtual park tours and route planners would make it more interactive. Additionally, adding an accessibility statement that explains how the website meets WCAG standards would show a commitment to inclusive design.

Conclusion

The SANParks website does a good job supporting tourism and conservation in South Africa and provides a good user experience. However, it could improve in areas like fairness, inclusion, and transparency. Fixing accessibility issues, adding clearer data protection messages, and offering more language options would make it better for all users. These changes would help the website meet higher ethical standards and set a good example of responsible design.
