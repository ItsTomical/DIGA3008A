Exploring the Web: From Memex to My First GitHub Page

17 February 2025

A personal reflection on learning to publish a website with GitHub Pages, exploring the early visions of the internet by Vannevar Bush, and tracing the web’s transformation from text-heavy systems to the dynamic platform we know today.

My experience setting up GitHub and publishing to Pages

Setting up GitHub and publishing to Pages was most definitely a learning experience. I would not have been able to publish to Pages (even with some prior knowledge of GitHub) were it not for the guide, which helped me set up the process. However, I must add that I also struggled with the guide at one point. So, my experience was that it was not as easy as I imagined it should be.

Reflection on the readings you selected - relating it to your experience with the internet today

As We May Think - Bush, 1945

When choosing to read As We May Think by Bush (1945), I initially struggled to understand its main focus. I had to go over it a few times to fully understand what Bush was saying. Eventually, I began to understand his ideas about technology and knowledge, especially his concept of the “Memex”, a system for storing and accessing information. He predicted a future with digital libraries, personal devices, and links between ideas. Today, I see these ideas reflected in platforms like Wikipedia (digital libraries), hyperlinks or hypertext (links between ideas), and devices like smartphones or computers (personal devices).

Bush highlighted the problem of information overload, where the enormous amount of knowledge available makes it difficult to find information that is relevant to what you are looking for. I think this problem is more of an issue now than ever before, even with tools like Google and Wikipedia. This is because the internet is so vast and it can be overwhelming at times. I often experience this problem myself. For example, when searching for resources for an assignment, I find that finding relevant information can be a lot of work. It becomes even more difficult when the topic is niche, even though there is such a wealth of knowledge available.

Bush also spoke about how machines would take over tasks and perform them at speeds that humans could not do manually, allowing us to focus more on creativity and problem solving rather than on the repetitive tasks. In today’s day and age, I think we can see Bush’s idea reflected in technologies like AI (such as ChatGPT) and search engines (like Google), and even smart assistants (like Siri), which help us process information faster and much more efficiently.

In conclusion, Bush’s ideas from 1945 are still relevant today. Modern tools do more than just store information; they help us to organise, find, and use knowledge more easily, just as Bush imagined.

History of the Web & A Little History of the World Wide Web :

Reading about The History of the Web and A Little History of the World Wide Web, looking at the timeline, I realise how much the internet has changed from then to now. From starting as text-based systems where people had to look for information manually, often using books or encyclopedias, to the creation of the World Wide Web by Tim Berners-Lee in 1989, and the development of the first browser by Mosaic in 1993. Now I feel as we begin making our own websites, it starts off like this, text-based and then gradually improving it with visuals, etc.

Interesting things I have learned about the web

What I find interesting about the history of the internet is how much the internet has changed over time from being so text-based. It is easy to forget that the internet we use today, with all its images, videos and interactive features, did not start like that. At first, everything focused on text and sharing basic information. I understand that websites were more like digital libraries, and it took a lot of time and effort to find what you were looking for. But as new technologies like the World Wide Web and web browsers were developed, we have been able to add links, images, and videos, changing how we use and experience information. This has made the internet much easier to use. What I also find interesting is that this way of sharing information was predicted in 1945 by Vannevar Bush, who imagined a system where information was linked together. The internet today is much more exciting, and it shapes our lives, from how we communicate to how we work and entertain ourselves.

  3. To better understand the workings of the internet

  5. To develop problem-solving skills
  6. To create a portfolio that demonstrates my skills I learn throughout this course
